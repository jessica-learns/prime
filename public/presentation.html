The Problem

The default output is the statistical middle — fluent, confident, and exactly what everyone else is getting.

AI can produce exceptional work, but only if you guide it there. Most people don't.

Why This Happens
LLMs are prediction engines. When you ask them to leap to a final answer, they land on the most statistically common one — which may be wrong. When you ask them to reason through intermediate steps, each step constrains the next, and the final answer is dramatically more accurate.

The default is what everyone gets. The techniques are what separate you.

The Context Window

More is not better — signal density matters.

The context window is everything the AI can see in one conversation — your instructions, your documents, the full chat history. It determines which parts of the LLM’s knowledge activate.

Finite capacity — 200K tokens standard for Claude (up to 1M on newer models), ~128K for ChatGPT. Exceed it and the oldest content gets dropped.
Position matters — Instructions at the top and your latest message get the most attention. Content buried in the middle can get lost.
Everything competes — 50 pages of irrelevant docs dilute the attention paid to the relevant ones. High signal in, high quality out.
Nothing persists — Every new conversation starts empty. Projects exist to reload the right tokens automatically.

Fill the window with signal, not noise. That’s what separates expert users from everyone else.

06 / 21
Your AI Toolkit

Two habits that set up every conversation for expert-level output.

LLM Project Folder ≠ Desktop Folder
A Project loads your instructions + reference documents into the context window before every conversation. Those tokens become part of the input, which steers the model’s attention patterns and determines which knowledge gets surfaced.
In the project folder instructions, you specify:
Role — “SMID-cap SaaS analyst” activates different expert pathways than “helpful assistant.”
Objective — A clear goal stops the model from hedging and covering everything.
Audience — Controls depth and vocabulary. A board gets a different answer than the engineering team.
Output format — LLMs fill a template more accurately than they generate freeform. Structure constrains each section, which constrains the next.
Constraints — Saying what you don’t want suppresses irrelevant pathways, which simultaneously boosts the ones you do. Higher signal, less noise.

Set these up once. Every conversation starts smarter.

07 / 21
Your AI Toolkit (Continued)

Two habits that improve every prompt you send.

Prompt step-by-step
When you ask the model to leap to a final answer, it lands on the most statistically common one. When you ask for intermediate steps, each step constrains the next — one technique tripled accuracy (Wei et al., 2022).
Upload your sources
Documents in the context window get used. Everything else gets guessed about. Upload your real materials and the model stops pattern-matching and starts reasoning from evidence.

These four habits work in any AI tool — chatbots, IDEs, agents. Learn them once, use them everywhere.

You just learned what separates the top 10% of AI users from everyone else.
